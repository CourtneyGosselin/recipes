## Ingredients

- 1 whole chicken (about 3.5–4 lbs)
- 4 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp fresh thyme leaves (or 1 tsp dried)
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
- Zest of 1 lemon
- 1 lemon, halved
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tbsp olive oil

## Instructions

1. Take the chicken out of the fridge 30 minutes before cooking. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).

2. Mix the butter with garlic, thyme, rosemary, and lemon zest. Season generously with salt and pepper.

3. Pat the chicken completely dry with paper towels — this is the key to crispy skin. Season the cavity with salt and pepper, then stuff with the lemon halves.

4. Carefully loosen the skin over the breasts by sliding your fingers underneath. Push most of the herb butter under the skin, spreading it evenly. Rub the remaining butter all over the outside.

5. Tie the legs together with kitchen twine. Drizzle with olive oil and season the outside generously with salt.

6. Place breast-side up in a roasting pan or cast iron skillet. Roast for 60–75 minutes, until the juices run clear when you pierce the thigh and a thermometer reads 165°F (74°C) at the thickest part.

7. Rest for at least 10–15 minutes before carving. Don't skip this.

## Pan Sauce (optional but worth it)

After removing the chicken, pour off most of the fat from the pan. Deglaze with ½ cup white wine or chicken stock, scraping up all the browned bits. Simmer for 2 minutes and season to taste.

---

> **Tip:** For the crispiest skin, air-dry the seasoned chicken uncovered in the fridge overnight.
