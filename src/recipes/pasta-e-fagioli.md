description: A rustic Italian bean and pasta soup — thick, hearty, and better the next day.

## Ingredients

- 3 tbsp olive oil, plus more to serve
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 stalks celery, diced
- 1 medium carrot, diced
- 1 sprig fresh rosemary
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- Pinch of red pepper flakes
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
- 2 cans (15 oz each) cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
- 4 cups chicken or vegetable stock
- 1 cup ditalini or small pasta
- Salt and black pepper
- Parmesan rind (optional but wonderful)
- Fresh parsley and grated Parmesan to serve

## Instructions

1. Heat olive oil in a large heavy pot over medium heat. Add onion, celery, and carrot with a pinch of salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, until softened — about 8 minutes.

2. Add garlic, rosemary, oregano, and red pepper flakes. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.

3. Stir in the tomato paste and cook 2 minutes, until it darkens slightly.

4. Add crushed tomatoes, beans, stock, and the Parmesan rind if using. Bring to a simmer. Cook 20 minutes.

5. Remove the rosemary sprig. Using a ladle, scoop out about 2 cups of the soup and blend until smooth, then stir back in. This gives the soup its thick, creamy body.

6. Bring back to a simmer and add the pasta. Cook until pasta is al dente, about 8–10 minutes. Season generously with salt and pepper.

7. Serve in bowls with a drizzle of good olive oil, fresh parsley, and a heavy snowfall of Parmesan.

---

> **Note:** The soup will thicken significantly as it sits. Add a splash of stock when reheating.
