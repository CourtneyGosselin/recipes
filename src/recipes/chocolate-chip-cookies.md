## Ingredients

- 2 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
- ¾ cup granulated sugar
- ¾ cup packed brown sugar
- 2 large eggs, room temperature
- 2 tsp pure vanilla extract
- 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.

2. Whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt in a medium bowl. Set aside.

3. In a large bowl, beat the butter and both sugars together on medium speed until light and fluffy, about 3–4 minutes. Don't rush this step — it's what gives the cookies their texture.

4. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Beat in the vanilla.

5. Reduce mixer speed to low and gradually add the flour mixture, mixing just until combined. Fold in the chocolate chips with a spatula.

6. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheets, spacing them about 2 inches apart.

7. Bake for 9–11 minutes, until the edges are golden but the centers still look slightly underdone. They'll continue to cook on the pan.

8. Let cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

---

> **Tip:** Refrigerate the dough for at least 30 minutes (or up to 72 hours) before baking for a richer, more complex flavour and better texture.
