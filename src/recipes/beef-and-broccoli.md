## Ingredients

### Beef
- Sirloin steak, sliced thin
- 1/4 cup cornstarch
- 1 tsp baking soda

### Sauce
- 1/2 cup soy sauce
- 1/2 cup water
- 3/4 cup brown sugar
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp ginger
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- MSG to taste

### Rest
- 2 broccoli heads (or 2 cups frozen broccoli)
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp onion powder
- Green onions, sliced (to serve)

## Instructions

1. Add the sliced steak, cornstarch, and baking soda to a zip-top bag. Seal and toss to coat. Set aside.

2. **Make the sauce:** In a medium saucepan, heat 1 tbsp olive oil and the sesame oil over medium-high. Add garlic and ginger, cook about 1 minute until fragrant — don't let it scorch. Add the soy sauce, water, and brown sugar. Stir until sugar dissolves, bring to a boil for 2–3 minutes, then reduce heat and simmer 5 minutes until slightly thickened.

3. In a large skillet, heat the remaining 2 tbsp olive oil over high heat. Add the steak and cook 5–7 minutes, flipping pieces occasionally for even browning.

4. Add the broccoli to the skillet.

5. Pour the sauce over everything — it will bubble up. Toss to coat and simmer on medium-high for 3–5 minutes until broccoli is crisp-tender.

6. Serve over rice, topped with sliced green onions.

---

**If the sauce is too thin:** Mix 1 tbsp cornstarch with 1 tbsp water and stir into the skillet for about 1 minute until it thickens to your liking. This is especially useful if you used a lot of broccoli, as it releases water while cooking.
